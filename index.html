Reach Us

Real Skills

Traditional high schools and universities are all have great teachers! They teach math, science, history, engineering, psychology, and about a hundred other subjects. However, most schools fail to teach students about real life skills like how to manage their time efficiently, managing their money, filing taxes, and understanding priorities.

Youth Lab offers real-life skills learning and career pathways classes where students learn how to manage their daily routine and building future Career Pathways. Prioritizing spending time early on to build career pathways for student is very vital. Teaching financial knowledge and learning how others manage money helps student to build their short and long term investment plan. Making financial spreadsheets, learning about finance stream, optimize income-expenses and build a sustainable future plan gives student more positive feelings for future.

Real-life learning program also incorporates teaching students individual budgets, giving them exposure about various financial toolkits including how to work in a team environment where you may encounter in the future to manage and work with difficult people

Students under the real-life learning program will learn how to manage their home, personal and professional life, expenses, people, and improving critical skills to stay ahead in life and career.

There is no such thing as a quick fix, but improving and learning new skills will support students' employment possibilities and what we expect workforce demands in the future. Learning the rules of life, how to build a career, or turn an idea into an entrepreneurial enterprise is essential.

The curriculum will be aligned with as many lessons and competencies as possible, so students stay focused for now and future goals. Students' capacities, budgets, and resources with real-life challenges must be considered and smartly planned.

The curriculum frameworks are consistent. However, the change may be faster than we expect in the coming years about the future of jobs, individualized goals, and more to develop customized career goals.

The real-world competencies development, advanced job planning, and career mapping will provide students the tools to plan a long-term career path. Students will explore their options of careers that align with their interests and passions.

Assessment tools are the first step to identifying students' interests and skills for their potential jobs. Lastly, job-shadowing, internships, and training will build students capacity to find their dream job.
